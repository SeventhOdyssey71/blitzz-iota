## Trading

### What is slippage?

<div style={{
  background: 'rgba(0, 255, 255, 0.1)',
  border: '1px solid rgba(0, 255, 255, 0.3)',
  borderRadius: '8px',
  padding: '16px',

}}>
  Slippage is the difference between expected and executed price due to:
  
  - Market movements during transaction
  - Large trade impact on pool price
  - Other trades executing first
  
  Set slippage tolerance to protect against unfavorable price changes.
</div>

### Why did my transaction fail?

Common reasons:
1. **Insufficient balance**: Check token amounts and gas
2. **Slippage exceeded**: Increase tolerance or reduce size
3. **Pool imbalance**: Try smaller amount or different pool
4. **Network congestion**: Retry with higher gas

### What are multi-hop swaps?

When no direct pool exists between two tokens, the router finds the best path through intermediate tokens:

```
Token A ‚Üí IOTA ‚Üí USDC ‚Üí Token B
```

This happens automatically and transparently.

## Liquidity Pools

### What is impermanent loss?

<div style={{
  background: 'rgba(255, 193, 7, 0.1)',
  border: '1px solid rgba(255, 193, 7, 0.3)',
  borderRadius: '8px',
  padding: '16px',
  marginTop: '16px',
  marginBottom: '16px'
}}>
  <strong>‚ö†Ô∏è Important Concept:</strong>
  
  Impermanent loss occurs when token price ratios change after you provide liquidity. The greater the divergence, the higher the IL.
  
  Example: If one token 2x while the other stays flat, you experience ~5.7% IL.
  
  It's "impermanent" because it only realizes if you withdraw at unfavorable ratios.
</div>

### How are LP rewards calculated?

Rewards come from:
1. **Trading fees**: 0.25% of all swaps
2. **Your pool share**: Percentage of total liquidity
3. **Additional incentives**: If applicable

Formula: `Your Rewards = (Total Fees √ó Your Pool Share)`

### Can I lose money providing liquidity?

Risks include:
- Impermanent loss if prices diverge
- Smart contract risk (mitigated by audits)
- Token depreciation

However, fee earnings often offset IL over time.

## Limit Orders

### How do limit orders execute?

<div style={{
  background: 'rgba(10, 10, 10, 0.8)',
  border: '1px solid rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.1)',
  borderRadius: '8px',
  padding: '16px',
  marginTop: '16px',
  marginBottom: '16px'
}}>
  Execution process:
  
  1. You set target price and amount
  2. Order waits in on-chain order book
  3. Keeper network monitors prices
  4. When price is met, keeper executes
  5. You receive tokens at exact price
</div>

### Are there fees for cancelled orders?

No fees for cancellation, only gas costs for the cancel transaction.

### Why hasn't my limit order filled?

Possible reasons:
- Market price hasn't reached your limit
- Insufficient liquidity at your price
- Order expired (if time-limited)
- Gas price too low for keepers
